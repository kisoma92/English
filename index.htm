What differences between countable and uncountable

Countable nouns have a plural form but uncountable noun don't

Example of countable nouns

  • One apple
  • Two apples

Example of uncountable nouns

  • Lump of sugar
  • Two spoonfuls of sugar

We can use a or an before a countable nouns but not before uncountable nouns

For example

a bean,An apple but not sugar, sunlight

Countable and uncountable nouns and quantifiers

A quantifiers is a word or a number of words,that describes the quantity or number of something.When we say a lump of sugar,we described the amount 'a lump of Suger'we described the amount of sugar by saying 'a lump of'.We use words like much,more,and many, enough and too little,any or some and all and no to talk about amounts and numbers.These words are all quantifiers

  • Quantifiers come before the noun or noun phrase they quantity
  • Which quantifiers come before the noun or noun phrase they quantify.uncountable:for example.we say much money but many coins
  • Some quantifier can be used with both types on nouns.They are : enough,more,some,alof /lots of,any ,all and no
  • Some quantifiers can only be used with countable nouns:few/a few/fewer/many/each and every,both
  • Other quantifiers can only be used with uncountable nouns:little/a little,less.It's really down to practice.The more you practice the more confident you will be at using quantifiers correctly

    What the meaning of Verb

    Verbs are words that show an action of the subject.Verbs are an important part of any language.The most basic part of any English sentence.

    The are two main types of verb.These are

    • Transitive verb
    • Intransitive verb

    Transitive verbs

    These are verb that containing direct object such as noun and pronoun

    For example

    The kid play football

    I going to buy car now

    That really joking me

    .

    Intransitive verb

    These are verbs that not show object direct

    For example

    His dog died suddenly last night

    Two boys were singing loudly

    Also one verb can contain both types of verb (Transitive and intransitive) uses

    For example

    They are singing songs

    They have been playing football evening

    Also a transitive verb can have inderect object such as pronoun or noun phrase that comes before a direct object and indicate the person or thing that receives what is done

    For example

    Can you give me a pen

    Who told her this information

    Note

    • The subject of every sentence must have a verb
    • The verb has to agree with the subject (for example,I am but He is )
    • A verb either tells us about an action the subject is performing (for example,She's singing) or about the condition or state(for example Our class is big)
    • A verb and its tense tells us about the time of an event/action or condition.Often it also tell us if the event/action is short, happens often or carries on for longer time

    Forming verbs

    Verb endings depend on the number or person

    • In the present tense ,verbs have different endings depending on the number or person used.For example,we say:I sing at the concert.He sings in the shower.We never sing together.
    • Most verbs in simple preseny tense just get an -s ending for 3rd person singular nouns like man, woman,book and 3rd person singular pronoun he,she and it
    • Depending on the verb,the 3rd person singular ending can change slightly.In verbs ending on an -o,we add-es for the 3rd person singular.For example I go to school.She goes to the theatre.We go to bed at end of this day
    • In verbs ending on an -s,-sh,-ch,-z or -x, we also add -es for the 3rd person singular For example I watch TV.She watches the rugby matchWe watch the girls the put up tent
    • In verbs ending on a consonant followed by -y,We change the -y to an i and add ed for the 3rd person singular.For example ,I worry about my exams a lof.He never worries but they worry like me

    What is modal verbs?

    Modal verbs are auxiliary verbs that show a person's ability or intention to do something.The modal verbs we mostly use are can,will and must,as well as may. Example,we use can to show abiliy:I can read and write but my baby brother can't.We use will to show that we intend to do something:He will learn to drive

    • Modal Verbs do not change their endings depending on the number or person,for example I can you can he/she,/it can ,we can ,you can ,they can
    • Modal verb are used together with another verb,For example: I can+swim,he/she/it can swim,he/she/it can swim(singular)
    • To form the negative ,we use the negative of the modal verb and add the base verb,for example.I can't dive,you can't dive,they can't dive,I will not/won't lie,..he will not /won't lie.

    Noun- verb agreement

    On page 4 you read that the verb has to agree with the subject

    The subject of sentence is the person or thing that does an action.

    For example

    • The girl speaks English,the subject.is the girl because she is the one who does the action of speaking English

    Here are some simple rules for noun-verb agreement

    • We check if the subject is 1st,2nd 3rd person singular or plural.you have learnt that with most present tense verbs,the 3rd person singular (he/she/it ) has a different ending to the verb for all other person.This Rule is the same for pronoun as for nouns
    • We decide if the subject of the sentence is singular or plural.This is normally easy,but what do we do in cases like

    Anita and Mei _____ to the market. .We say Anita goes to the market.and Mei goes to the market.Anita and Mei are two people.=>Anita and mei(they) go to the market.

    Anita or Mei ____ here.We say Anita is her and Mei is here.The word or means that it either Anita or Mei but not both of them Therefore we say Anita or Mei is here

    Neither Anita nor Mei_______to go(want).We say Neither Anita or Mei wants to go . Neither one of them wants to go

    • Some subjects have two nouns,where one of noun is plural and the other is singular,

      • For example

      Haji and his sisters_____to learn Portuguese.(want)

    • We use the plural verb form Haji and his sisters want to learn Portuguese.They want learn it
    • Because his sisters are mentioned last ,and becausehis sisters is plural subject,we also use the plural form want(not wants)

      Either Haji or his sisters wants to learn kiswahili

      Neither Haji nor his sister want to learn Dutch

    • When a sentence has two different pronouns,it sometimes becomes a bit tricky.. Normally we use the verb form for the pronoun last mentioned in the subject

      • For example

      He or I know the answer.She or they go . Neither you nor I want to go

    • When we talk aboutva whole group of things or people, like:crowd of people or a herd of cattle ,We usually use the singular verb form
